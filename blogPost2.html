2017 Draft Thoughts

By: Collin Joselyn

The 2017 first year player draft was a big opportunity for the Twins. They were getting the first overall pick, the first time since 2001 that they got the first overall pick. Going into the draft I was certain that the Twins would pick Hunter Greene, pitcher out of Notre Dame High School in California, with a 102 mph fastball. But instead they shocked everyone and picked Royce Lewis, a shortstop from JSerra Catholic High School in San Juan Capistrano, California. This was shocking because Royce was not even predicted to go in the top 10. I was kind of skeptical of this because we have the first pick so lets get the best available player. We are also in need of an ace. I understand the risk of drafting pitchers in the first round because their arms do not last long. We already have an abundant supply of good position players. This organization is afraid to pull the trigger and draft a pitcher. Possibly because our minor league system has failed to develop an ace. For example guys like Kyle Gibson and Alex Wimmers were first round picks for the Twins. They have not lived up to expectations. Kyle Gibson was aweful in the biggining of 2017 but then turned it around later in the season after being designated for assignment and later brought back. Once thought of a being our next pitching ace, is now mainly considered a 4th or 5th man in the rotation. But in conclusion we will have to wait for the future to come and see how Hunter and Royce turn out.
